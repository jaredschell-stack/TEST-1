## SAFETY PRECAUTIONS
**WARNING**: Wear respirator when using 360-4 metal cleaner, mixing, or spraying primer/sealer.  
**WARNING**: Wear dust mask and safety glasses when sanding.  
**WARNING**: Wear safety glasses or face shield when using abrasive wheel.  
**CAUTION**: Ensure vehicle is securely supported on lift or rotisserie to prevent falling.  
**NOTE**: Label all hardware and parts for reassembly. Take photos with PO# after each major step.

## PROCESS 1 – CHEMICAL CLEANING
**WARNING**: Wear respirator when using 360-4 metal cleaner.

1. **BODY PREP.**  
   a. Wipe off all markings from metal shop with lacquer thinner and shop towel.  
   b. Wipe off primer on all panels with lacquer thinner.  

2. **HOOD.**  
   a. Use metal cleaner (360-4) with red scuff pad on bare metal.  
   b. Wipe with Wax & Grease Remover.  

3. **HOOD VENTS.**  
   a. Use metal cleaner (360-4) with red scuff pad on bare metal.  
   b. Wipe with Wax & Grease Remover.  

4. **COWL.**  
   a. Use metal cleaner (360-4) with red scuff pad on bare metal.  
   b. Wipe with Wax & Grease Remover.  

5. **FRONT FENDERS.**  
   a. Use metal cleaner (360-4) with red scuff pad on bare metal.  
   b. Wipe with Wax & Grease Remover.  

6. **DOORS.**  
   a. Use metal cleaner (360-4) with red scuff pad on bare metal.  
   b. Wipe with Wax & Grease Remover.  

7. **QUARTER PANEL.**  
   a. Use metal cleaner (360-4) with red scuff pad on bare metal.  
   b. Wipe with Wax & Grease Remover.  

8. **TRUNK LID AND REAR COWL.**  
   a. Use metal cleaner (360-4) with red scuff pad on bare metal.  
   b. Wipe with Wax & Grease Remover.  

9. **FRONT & REAR VALANCES.**  
   a. Use metal cleaner (360-4) with red scuff pad on bare metal.  
   b. Wipe with Wax & Grease Remover.  

10. **QUARTER PANEL EXTENSIONS.**  
    a. Use metal cleaner (360-4) with red scuff pad on bare metal.  
    b. Wipe with Wax & Grease Remover.  

11. **DOOR JAMBS.**  
    a. Use metal cleaner (360-4) with red scuff pad on bare metal.  
    b. Wipe with Wax & Grease Remover.  

12. **DASH.**  
    a. Use metal cleaner (360-4) with red scuff pad on bare metal.  
    b. Wipe with Wax & Grease Remover.  

## PROCESS 2 – SANDING AND ABRASIVE WORK
**WARNING**: Wear dust mask and safety glasses when sanding.

1. **HOOD.**  
   a. Strip E-coating with 80-grit sanding discs on DA.  

2. **HOOD VENTS.**  
   a. Strip to bare metal with 80-grit sandpaper.  

3. **COWL.**  
   a. Strip E-coat with 80-grit sandpaper and sanding discs on DA.  
   b. Sand between grille openings.  

4. **FRONT FENDERS.**  
   a. Strip E-coating with 80-grit sanding discs on DA.  

5. **DOORS.**  
   a. Strip E-coating with 80-grit sanding discs on DA.  
   b. Strip front edge of quarter panel where it meets rear of door with 80-grit sanding discs on DA.  
   c. Strip interior side of door with 80-grit sandpaper.  
   d. Sand inner edge of window opening down past trim slits with 80-grit sandpaper by hand.  

6. **ROOF.**  
   a. Strip E-coating off entire roof and A-pillars with 80-grit sanding discs on DA.  

7. **FRONT & REAR VALANCES.**  
   a. Strip E-coating with 80-grit sanding discs on DA.  

8. **QUARTER PANEL.**  
   a. Strip E-coating with 80-grit sanding discs on DA.  
   b. Strip trunk lid and rear cowl with 80-grit sanding discs on DA.  

9. **DOOR JAMBS.**  
   a. Strip E-coating with abrasive wheel and 80-grit sandpaper past hinge mounting holes.  

10. **DASH.**  
    a. Strip E-coating from middle down with 80-grit sandpaper on DA and by hand.  

11. **TRUNK.**  
    a. Strip E-coating around/in trunk sill area with 80-grit sandpaper and abrasive wheel.  

12. **FINAL PAINT PREP – ALL PANELS.**  
    a. Apply guide coat.  
    b. Block sand with 320-grit sandpaper in cross-hatch pattern.  
    c. Sand with DA and intermediate pad or by hand.  
    d. Sand body lines and edges with red scuff pad and 360-grit sandpaper.  
    e. Repeat with 400-grit sandpaper.  
    f. Scuff E-coating on headlight buckets with red scuff pad.  
    g. Sand hard-to-reach areas with red scuff pad and 360-grit sandpaper.  
    h. Sand interior side of door with 320-grit on DA or by hand.  
    i. Sand inside of hood structure with 320-grit by hand and DA.  
    j. Sand valances with 320-grit on DA or by hand.  
    k. Sand back side of valances with 320-grit by hand and DA.  
    l. Sand quarter panel vents, hood vents with 400-grit by hand.  
    m. Sand door hinges with 180-grit on DA or by hand until E-coat dull.  
    n. Sand taillight panel with 320-grit on DA with intermediate pad or by hand.  
    o. Sand cowl with 320-grit on DA with intermediate pad or by hand.  
    p. Sand firewall (painted) with 320-grit on DA with intermediate pad or by hand.  
    q. Sand fender extensions with 320-grit on DA with intermediate pad or by hand.  
    r. Sand quarter extensions with 320-grit on DA with intermediate pad or by hand.  

## PROCESS 3 – SPRAYING EPOXY SEALER AND PRIMER
**WARNING**: Wear respirator when mixing and spraying primer/sealer.

1. **BODY: EPOXY SEALER & PRIMER.**  
   a. Tape off engine bay, core support, insides of fenders.  
   b. Back mask front/rear windshields and door openings.  
   c. Mask off suspension and wheels.  
   d. Wipe bare metal with metal etch wipes.  
   e. Wipe areas to be primed with Wax & Grease Remover.  
   f. Mix and spray one coat of epoxy sealer.  
   g. Mix and spray four coats of high-build primer.  
   h. Block sand body and panels with 320-grit sandpaper.  
   i. Repair issues: sand with 180-grit, spot prime, sand with 320-grit.  

2. **EPOXY SEALER & PRIMER: JAMBS INTERIOR.**  
   a. Spray dash first.  
   b. Tape off previously primed areas.  
   c. Back mask door jambs and engine bay.  
   d. Wipe bare metal with metal etch wipes.  
   e. Back mask door edges.  
   f. Mix and spray one coat of epoxy sealer.  
   g. Mix and spray four coats of high-build primer.  
   h. Block sand doors, firewall, jambs with 320-grit sandpaper.  
   i. Repair issues: sand with 180-grit, spot prime, sand with 320-grit.  

## PROCESS 4 – REMAINING OPERATIONS (NO SAFETY WARNINGS REQUIRED)

### PREPARATION
1. **REVIEW BUILD SHEET.**  
   a. Review build sheet with Manager or team lead.  

2. **DOCUMENT VEHICLE.**  
   a. Take 360° photos of entire car including gaps, panel fitment, parts.  

3. **REMOVE AND STORE PARTS.**  
   a. Remove parts not needed for gapping.  
   b. Label parts.  
   c. Place in two totes: one for hardware/small items, one for larger wrapped parts.  
   d. Notify supervisor of damaged parts.  

### FENDER EXTENSION
4. **REMOVE FENDER EXTENSIONS.**  
   a. Remove extensions from both fenders.  
   b. Remove headlight adjusters (two screws per adjuster).  
   c. Remove three mounting studs with nuts.  
   d. Bag and label hardware. Place in tote.  
   e. Sandblast both sides of extensions to bare aluminum.  

### HOOD
5. **PREPARE HOOD.**  
   a. Get Manager to inspect for dents/imperfections.  
   b. Remove three bolts from hood hinge. Place hood on body panel rack.  
   c. Remove hinges from firewall. Wrap in bubble wrap. Place in tote.  
   d. Bag hinge hardware. Place in tote.  
   e. Remove hood vents. Strip to bare metal with 80-grit sandpaper.  
   f. Get Manager to inspect.  

### COWL
6. **PREPARE COWL.**  
   a. Get Manager to inspect for dents/imperfections.  

### FRONT FENDERS
7. **PREPARE FENDERS.**  
   a. Get Manager to inspect for dents/imperfections.  
   b. Reinstall fender extensions for gap alignment. Match elevations.  
   c. Get Manager to inspect gaps. Approve body filler if needed.  
   d. Clean with Wax & Grease Remover on white cloth.  
   e. Apply body filler to areas required.  
   f. Pull plastic spreader edge up and around seam before filler hardens.  
   g. Manager must inspect seam before unbolting extension.  
   h. If “Good”, unbolt and lay on table.  
   i. Sand fender edge with 180-grit sandpaper.  
   j. Clean and add body filler to voids on fender edge. Remove tape before filler hardens.  
   k. Use sanding block with 80-grit and 180-grit. DO NOT HAND SAND.  
   l. Sculpt nice edge on fender.  
   m. Get Manager approval.  
   n. Take photos with PO#.  

### DOORS
8. **PREPARE DOORS.**  
   a. Get Manager to inspect for low spots/imperfections.  
   b. Apply body filler to areas required.  
   c. Sand with 180-grit sandpaper.  
   d. Get Manager to inspect for pinholes/additional work.  
   e. Take photos with PO#.  
   f. Ensure door rubbers installed prior to gap work.  
   g. Get Manager to inspect gaps. Approve body filler if needed.  
   h. Pull plastic spreader edge up to prevent losing gap before filler hardens.  
   i. Sand body filler with 80-grit and 180-grit.  
   j. Re-clean and add more filler if needed.  
   k. Apply guide coat. Sand until gone.  
   l. Get Manager to inspect for pinholes.  
   m. Take photos with PO#.  

### ROOF
9. **PREPARE ROOF.**  
   a. Get Manager to inspect A-pillars and roof for dents/imperfections.  
   b. Tape off drip rail prior to body filler.  
   c. Apply body filler to roof/A-pillar and roof/quarter panel joints.  
   d. Block sand roof/quarter panel joint to straighten body lines.  
   e. Apply guide coat once rough shape achieved.  
   f. Block sand until body lines defined.  
   g. Re-clean and add more filler if needed.  
   h. Get Manager approval.  
   i. Take photos with PO#.  

### FRONT & REAR VALANCES
10. **REMOVE AND PREPARE VALANCES.**  
    a. Remove front valance (six bolts: two per fender, one per frame rail).  
    b. Remove bolt clips. Bag hardware. Place in tote.  
    c. Remove rear valance (two bolts, sheet metal screws).  
    d. Bag hardware. Place in tote.  
    e. Place valances on bench or body stand.  
    f. Get Manager to inspect for dents/imperfections.  
    g. Fix imperfections. Apply body filler if needed.  
    h. Sand with 80-grit and 180-grit once hardened.  
    i. Re-clean and add more filler if needed.  
    j. Get Manager to inspect for pinholes.  
    k. Take photos with PO#.  

### QUARTER PANEL EXTENSIONS
11. **REMOVE AND PREPARE QUARTER EXTENSIONS.**  
    a. Remove extensions from quarter panels (three nuts/washers per side).  
    b. Sand E-coating until able to use metal cleaner.  

### QUARTER PANEL
12. **PREPARE QUARTER PANEL.**  
    a. Reinstall quarter panel extensions (second person to hold).  
    b. Match alignment with quarter panel (horizontal/vertical elevations).  
    c. Ensure trunk rubber weather stripping installed. Close trunk fully.  
    d. Get Manager to inspect fitment, low spots, dents.  
    e. Get Manager to inspect gaps. Approve body filler if needed.  
    f. Clean with Wax & Grease Remover on white cloth.  
    g. Apply body filler to areas required.  
    h. Pull plastic spreader edge up and around seam before filler hardens.  
    i. Block sand quarter panel/extension gap.  
    j. Re-clean and add more filler if needed.  
    k. Apply guide coat. Block sand until gone.  
    l. Get Manager to inspect for pinholes.  
    m. Manager must inspect seam before unbolting extension.  
    n. If “Good”, unbolt and lay on table.  
    o. Take photos with PO#.  
    p. Sand quarter panel where extension mounts with 180-grit sandpaper.  
    q. Clean and add body filler to voids on quarter panel edge.  
    r. Use sanding block with 80-grit and 180-grit. DO NOT HAND SAND.  
    s. Sculpt nice edge on quarter panel.  
    t. Get Manager approval.  
    u. Strip E-coating off taillight panel with 80-grit sanding discs on DA.  
    v. Get Manager to inspect panel for imperfections.  

### SEAM SEALER
13. **APPLY SEAM SEALER.**  
    a. Clean seams with Wax & Grease Remover.  
    b. Tape both sides of seams.  
    c. Apply blue Medium 123EZ two-part seam sealer.  
    d. Wipe smooth with gloved finger. Force into seam.  
    e. Remove tape before sealer hardens. Finger wipe again if needed.  
    f. Clean excess with lacquer thinner on white cloth (not in seams).  
    g. Get Manager to inspect work.  
    h. Apply to: Cowl, Door Jambs, Drip Rail, Taillight Panel, Quarter Vents, all Door seams, Cowl/firewall/fender apron joint, all trunk seams, rear cowl/quarter panel seam, drip rail top & bottom, quarter panel vent, three seams on door, cowl area covered by fender.  

### BODY & FRAME SEPARATION
14. **SEPARATE BODY FROM FRAME.**  
    a. Roll car to lift.  
    **SAFETY NOTE**: Ensure tub centered and balanced. Ask for help if unsure.  
    b. Move chassis/body to center of lift.  
    c. Position lift arms under frame rails with rubber pads.  
    d. Raise car to working height.  
    e. Label all body mount hardware locations for reassembly.  
    f. Remove two ¾” body bolts in trunk area.  
    g. Remove two 5/8” body bolts from rear rocker area.  
    h. Remove two 9/16” nuts from body mount.  
    i. Remove two turnbuckle mounts above rear axle (nuts inside car).  
    j. Remove lug nuts from front hubs. Remove Guni wheels.  
    k. Remove top bolt and spacer from ball joint (12-point 9/16” wrench top, wrench bottom).  
    l. Remove tie rod end nut. Swing tie rod out. Rethread nut.  
    m. Remove top coil-over bolt and spacers (with coworker support).  
    n. Swing lower control arm with hub and coil-over down.  
    o. Remove two ¾” bolts per side through frame with sleeve, washer, nut.  
    p. Remove eight 9/16” bolts (four engine bay, four wheel well) for upper control arm mount.  
    q. Lift and slide upper control arm and mount out. Repeat other side.  
    r. Place two jackstands under front K-member behind steering rack. Raise to suspend front suspension.  
    s. Place two jackstands under rear axle housing.  
    t. Lower car onto jackstands.  
    u. Position lift arms to lift body by pinch welds.  
    v. Ensure rubber pucks same height. Lift body evenly.  
    w. Lift body off frame.  
    x. Reassemble front suspension for rolling chassis.  
       **NOTE**: Hand tighten 12-point bolt first to avoid cross-threading.  
    y. Reinstall Guni wheels.  
    z. Remove jackstands. Lower chassis to ground.  
    aa. Roll chassis aside.  
    bb. Lift body to clear rotisserie.  
    cc. Roll rotisserie underneath.  
    dd. Lower body even with rotisserie arms.  
    ee. Slide front rotisserie arms into front frame rails (outside).  
    ff. Secure with 7/16” x 4” bolts, lock washer, large flat washer, nut.  
    gg. Secure rear arms underneath rear frame rails with 7/16” x 6.5” bolts, lock washer, large flat washer, two nuts as spacers.  
    hh. Tighten center bolt on rotisserie arms.  
    ii. Tighten six bolts per arm (three top, three bottom).  
    jj. Tighten center beam bolts.  
    kk. Lower lift. Slide arms out.  
    ll. Move rotisserie with body to Paint Shop.  

### FINAL PAINT PREP
15. **ROOF.**  
    a. Apply guide coat. Sand with 320-grit on DA with intermediate pad or by hand.  
    b. Fill pinholes with Finishing Glaze and razor blade. Sand once dry.  
    c. Sand corners/hard edges with red scuff pad and 360-grit.  
    d. Repeat with 400-grit.  
    e. Wet with Wax & Grease Remover. Must meet “Clean Towel” spec.  
    f. Get Manager to inspect for scratches/pinholes with LED light.  
    g. Perform additional work as directed.  
    h. After roof, A-pillars, drip rails approved, proceed to quarter panels and door jambs.  

16. **QUARTER PANEL.**  
    a. Apply guide coat.  
    b. Block sand with 320-grit in cross-hatch pattern, then DA with intermediate pad or by hand.  
    c. Sand body lines/edges with red scuff pad and 360-grit.  
    d. Repeat with 400-grit.  
    e. Wet with Wax & Grease Remover. Must meet “Clean Towel” spec.  
    f. Get Manager to inspect for scratches/pinholes with LED light.  
    g. Perform additional work as directed.  

17. **DOOR JAMBS.**  
    a. Wipe with Wax & Grease Remover.  
    b. Sand with 320-grit on DA and by hand for hard-to-reach areas.  
    c. Sand hard-to-reach areas/edges with red scuff pad and 360-grit.  
    d. Sand rear of jamb and top side of rocker/jamb with 400-grit.  
    e. Get Manager to inspect for scratches/pinholes with LED light.  
    f. Perform additional work as directed.  
    g. Sand inside door opening, A-pillar, front windshield with 320-grit on DA or by hand.  
    h. Wipe with Wax & Grease Remover.  
    i. Sand around rear windshield with 320-grit on DA or by hand.  
    j. Wipe with Wax & Grease Remover.  

18. **DASH.**  
    a. Wipe entire dash with Wax & Grease Remover.  
    b. Sand down with 320-grit on DA or by hand.  
    c. Sand edges/hard-to-reach with 360-grit and red scuff pad.  
    d. Sand primed section with 400-grit.  
    e. Get Manager to inspect for scratches/pinholes with LED light.  
    f. Perform additional work as directed.  

19. **GLOVE BOX DOOR.**

    b. Sand front and back with 320-grit on DA or by hand.  
    c. Sand front with 400-grit.  
    d. Wet with Wax & Grease Remover. Must meet “Clean Towel” spec.  
    e. Get Manager to inspect for scratches/pinholes with LED light.  
    f. Perform additional work as directed.

    i. Flip trunk lid face up.  
    j. Wet out with Wax & Grease Remover and white cloth.

30. **QUARTER EXTENSIONS.**  
    a. Sand with 320-grit on DA with intermediate pad or by hand.  
    b. Sand edges with red scuff pad and 360-grit.  
    c. Repeat with 400-grit.  
    d. Wet with Wax & Grease Remover. Must meet “Clean Towel” spec.  
    e. Get Manager to inspect for flaws.  
    f. Perform additional work as directed.  

### HARDWARE
31. **PREPARE HARDWARE FOR PAINT.**  
    a. Cut open bags to reuse after paint.  
    b. Pour hardware into mixing bucket with lacquer thinner. Agitate by hand.  
    c. Sandblast bolt heads in sandblasting cabinet.  
    d. Sand paint on cardboard with 80-grit.  
    e. Place bolts in cardboard holes.  
    f. Have Painters paint bolt heads.  
    g. Label bolt locations on cardboard back.  
    h. Attach PO# to cardboard with tape.  

### MASKING
32. **MASK INTERIOR AND OPENINGS.**  
    a. Blow out interior to remove dust/debris.  
    b. Tape off dash openings from back. Run duct tape along bottom edge.  
    c. Tape off holes under dash, firewall, cowl, door hinge holes.  
    d. Make 2” tape line under door opening front to back.  
    e. Tape up quarter panel vent and striker mount holes in jamb.  
    f. Run 36” paper along floor pan each side with overlap.  
    g. Run 36” paper under dash, tape 2-3” back from door opening.  
    h. Run paper down from back seat support. Tape to floor paper.  
    i. Paper off rear wheel wells and openings behind doors.  
    j. Tape off trunk pan across entire trunk.  
    k. Tape off taillight panel openings and holes.  
    l. Create tape line straight down fender apron.  
    m. Run duct tape up and around front of door and under cowl.  
    n. Wrap aprons and core support. Leave engine bay open for firewall paint.  
    o. Wrap all panels in plastic. Store.  
    p. Attach PO# to cardboard with tape.
